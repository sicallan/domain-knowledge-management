01

The problem

Your AI strategy is only as trustworthy as the knowledge beneath it — and right now, that's a wiki.

Every enterprise has an agentic mandate and a budget. But the agents stall the moment they touch the real domain, because the knowledge they depend on is:

  • Locked in prose — vendor docs, project specs, runbooks and tribal knowledge that no model can reliably reason over. An estimated [ ~80% ] of enterprise information is unstructured. [ source ]
  • Disconnected from reality — what the documentation says, what was actually built, and what runs in production have quietly drifted apart, so retrieval returns confident but wrong answers.
  • Impossible to audit — when an agent makes an assertion, no one can see the evidence, the decision logic, or the owner. In regulated domains, "the model said so" is not an answer.

The result: RAG-over-documents demos impress in a sandbox and then die in production at exactly the high-stakes decisions where the value — and the risk — concentrate.

Why it's urgent: the enterprises moving fastest on agents hit this knowledge wall first, and they hit it on their most valuable, most regulated workflows. [ Add 1 customer anecdote or a market stat with source ]
02

The solution

Truth, not text — a living, evidenced knowledge graph with decisions as first-class nodes.

Veritgraph continuously ingests an enterprise's source material — documents and operational telemetry — and structures it into a living knowledge graph. The core principle: this is not a document store. Every artifact must populate, update or evidence a typed inventory entry with explicit relationships.

  • The document is the evidence; the entry is the assertion; the graph connects assertions; views interpret it; agents reason over it — with the source for every claim.
  • Decisions are first-class. Where logic selects an outcome from inputs, rules, reference data and invariants — the points where regulation bites and errors are costliest — are modelled explicitly, not buried in paragraphs.
  • Everything is evidenced (linked to source provenance) and bi-temporally versioned — auditable and time-travellable from day one.

❌ RAG on documents

Hands the agent text. No structure, no relationships, no evidence, no decision logic — confident answers nobody can verify.

✅ Veritgraph

Hands the agent a typed, evidenced subgraph: the concepts and decisions, the rules and data behind them, and the provenance for every assertion.

04

Product & technology

Defensible by architecture — the hard parts are built in, not bolted on.

Veritgraph is anchored in an opinionated, four-layer Domain-Driven-Design model. One canonical truth that everything else maps to, so you can compare what was promised, what was specified, and what actually runs:

LayerWhat it captures
L0 · StrategicInitiatives, value streams, roadmaps — the "why"
L1 · Pure DomainCanonical truth — domains, bounded contexts, concepts & decisions (vendor-agnostic)
L2 · FunctionalVendor products & project specs that claim to fulfil L1
L3 · TechnicalSystems, services, runtime behaviour & operational evidence

Decisions-first ontology

Decisions modelled as the highest-value nodes, wired to the rules, reference data and invariants that govern them.

Extract once, load many

Any source becomes typed, schema-validated records once, then fans out to graph, vector and relational stores. New sources/targets plug in without touching the core (open–closed).

Evidence + bi-temporal lineage

Every assertion carries provenance and a validity window — built for audit and regulation.

Agent-native retrieval

Typed subgraphs and purpose-built views feed copilots, analysts and builder-agents — and can generate specs/services from canonical truth.

Moat: the ontology, evidence model, temporal lineage and extensibility compound over time and are hard to retrofit onto a flat document index. [ note any patents / proprietary datasets / prompts ]
